Movies Like Mother's Day (2023)

March 11, 2023

Mother’s Day is a special day to honor and appreciate mothers worldwide. In 2023, the film industry is set to release some exciting movies with themes that celebrate motherhood. If you’re looking for movies like Mother’s Day (2023), here are some recommendations to consider:

1. Mamma Mia! Here We Go Again (2018)

If you loved the all-star cast of Mother’s Day, you’ll enjoy Mamma Mia! Here We Go Again. This movie is a sequel to the 2008 hit musical, Mamma Mia!, and features a star-studded cast, including Meryl Streep, Pierce Brosnan, and Amanda Seyfried. The movie tells the story of Sophie, who learns more about her mother’s past while preparing for the grand reopening of the hotel.

2. Little Women (2019)

Little Women is a heartwarming adaptation of the classic novel by Louisa May Alcott. The movie tells the story of four sisters who grow up during the American Civil War. The film features an all-star cast, including Saoirse Ronan, Emma Watson, and Timothee Chalamet.

3. Joy (2015)

Joy is a biographical drama film starring Jennifer Lawrence, Bradley Cooper, and Robert De Niro. The movie tells the story of Joy Mangano, a single mother who becomes a successful entrepreneur after inventing the Miracle Mop.

4. The Kids Are All Right (2010)

The Kids Are All Right is a comedy-drama film that tells the story of a lesbian couple, Nic and Jules, and their two teenage children. When their children seek out their biological father, it leads to unexpected changes in their family dynamic. The movie stars Julianne Moore, Annette Bening, and Mark Ruffalo.

5. Steel Magnolias (1989)

Steel Magnolias is a classic movie about a group of women who support each other through life’s ups and downs. The movie is set in a small Louisiana town and features an all-star cast, including Sally Field, Julia Roberts, and Dolly Parton.

6. The Help (2011)

The Help is a drama film set in the 1960s and tells the story of African American maids working in Mississippi. The movie stars Emma Stone, Viola Davis, and Octavia Spencer and explores themes of racism, discrimination, and friendship.

7. Lady Bird (2017)

Lady Bird is a coming-of-age comedy-drama film that tells the story of a teenage girl growing up in Sacramento, California. The movie stars Saoirse Ronan and explores themes of family, identity, and the transition to adulthood.

8. Stepmom (1998)

Stepmom is a heartwarming movie about a terminally ill mother who must come to terms with the fact that her ex-husband is moving on with a new woman. The film features an all-star cast, including Julia Roberts, Susan Sarandon, and Ed Harris.

9. My Big Fat Greek Wedding (2002)

My Big Fat Greek Wedding is a romantic comedy that tells the story of a Greek-American woman who falls in love with a non-Greek man. The movie stars Nia Vardalos and John Corbett and explores themes of family, tradition, and cultural identity.

10. The Blind Side (2009)

The Blind Side is a biographical sports drama film that tells the story of Michael Oher, a homeless and traumatized boy who becomes an All-American football player and first-round NFL draft pick. The movie stars Sandra Bullock, Tim McGraw, and Quinton Aaron.
