Mother’s Day 2016: A Look Back and Forward

The History of Mother’s Day

Mother’s Day has been celebrated for over a century, with the first official Mother’s Day in the United States being observed in 1914. The holiday was created by Anna Jarvis, who wanted to honor her own mother and all mothers. Today, Mother’s Day is celebrated in many countries around the world.

Mother’s Day 2016

In 2016, Mother’s Day fell on May 8th. Many people celebrated the day by giving their mothers gifts, cards, and flowers. Some families also went out to brunch or dinner to celebrate.

How Mother’s Day Has Changed

Over the years, Mother’s Day has evolved to become more than just a day to honor mothers. It has become a commercial holiday, with many businesses offering Mother’s Day specials and discounts. There has also been a shift towards celebrating all types of mother figures, including stepmothers, grandmothers, and even friends who are mothers.

Mother’s Day 2023

Looking ahead to 2023, Mother’s Day will fall on May 14th. While it is impossible to predict exactly how the holiday will be celebrated in seven years, it is likely that the trend towards celebrating all types of mother figures will continue. Additionally, as technology continues to advance, it is possible that more people will opt to send e-cards or other digital gifts instead of traditional cards and flowers.
